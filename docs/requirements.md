# Requirements

## Goal

Build a personal accounting system that records each expense once, while separately managing:

- budget usage by consumption date;
- cash-flow movement by payment date;
- income timing by income date;
- future credit-card and installment payment pressure.

The system must support a private GitHub repository for specifications, templates, rules, anonymized samples, and later implementation code. Real Excel accounting files remain local.

## Core Principle

Consumption date deducts budget. Payment date deducts cash flow.

Example: a February credit-card purchase of 12,000 for daily supplies, paid in three installments, deducts 12,000 from the February budget item immediately. Cash flow is deducted across the generated payment dates.

## Sources

Expense sources:

- Ministry of Finance invoice details;
- manual entries for purchases without invoices, including overseas purchases.

Income sources:

- actual income records from the income workbook;
- salary structure assumptions for future income estimates;
- manually entered bonus dates.

## Budget Classification

Expense classification must use the `item` values from `2026Budget` in the annual budget workbook.

The system must not create free-form budget categories. The `預算總額` row is a summary row and must be excluded from valid expense items.

## Payment Tracking

Payment method is split into:

- `payment_tool_type`: cash or credit card;
- `credit_card_name`: YuShan, Union, Cathay, Fubon, CTBC, or another configured card.

Installments are generated by average split first, but each payment schedule entry may be manually corrected during reconciliation.

## Budget Alerts

The system tracks both annual and monthly budget usage by item.

Alerts do not block recording. They only warn.

Budget status thresholds:

- below 70 percent: normal;
- 70 percent to below 90 percent: reminder;
- 90 percent to below 100 percent: warning;
- 100 percent or above: over budget, highlighted most prominently.

## Cash Flow

The first version of cash-flow reporting uses:

```text
monthly net cash flow = monthly income - monthly payment expense
```

The first version does not include beginning cash balance, bank account balances, transfer planning, or debit account tracking.
